# Ready-Made Routines

Six complete programs built from the exercises in this guide. Pick **one** and run it for 8 to 12 weeks before switching. For all of them: warm up first (previous chapter), rest 60–180 s between sets depending on the exercise, and apply the load progression from the principles chapter.

---

## Routine 1 — Home, Beginner (full body, 3x/week)

No equipment. Mon / Wed / Fri (or every other day).

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|---|---|
| Bodyweight Squat | 3 × 12–15 |
| Incline Pushups (hands elevated) or Pushups | 3 × 8–12 |
| Glute Bridge (bodyweight) | 3 × 12–15 |
| Inverted Row (bodyweight)* | 3 × 8–12 |
| Plank | 3 × 20–40 s |
| Star Jumps | 2 × 30 s |

*No bar at home? Use a sturdy table, or swap in Supermans/floor back extensions.

**Progression:** once you hit the top of the rep range on every set, move up to a harder variation (e.g., incline pushup → standard → decline) or add 2 reps to the range.

---

## Routine 2 — Home, Intermediate (dumbbells, ABC 3–6x/week)

A pair of adjustable dumbbells covers everything.

**Workout A — Push:** Dumbbell Bench Press 4×8–12 · Standing Dumbbell Shoulder Press 3×8–12 · Flat Dumbbell Flyes 3×10–15 · Dumbbell Overhead Triceps Extension 3×10–15 · Close-Grip (Diamond) Pushups 2× max.

**Workout B — Pull:** One-Arm Dumbbell Row 4×8–12 · Bent Over Dumbbell Row 3×8–12 · Reverse Dumbbell Flyes 3×12–15 · Alternating Dumbbell Curls 3×10–12 · Hammer Curls 2×10–12.

**Workout C — Legs and Core:** Goblet Squat 4×8–12 · Dumbbell Lunges 3×10 per leg · Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift 3×10–12 · Standing Dumbbell Calf Raises 3×15–20 · Crunches 3×15 · Side Plank 2×20–30 s per side.

Cycle A → B → C and start over. 3 days/week = each workout once; 6 days = each workout twice.

## Routine 3 — Gym, Beginner (full body, 3x/week)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|---|---|
| Barbell Squat (or Leg Press) | 3 × 8–12 |
| Barbell Bench Press (or Machine) | 3 × 8–12 |
| Wide-Grip Lat Pulldown | 3 × 8–12 |
| Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press | 2 × 10–12 |
| Lying Leg Curl | 2 × 10–15 |
| Kneeling Cable Crunch | 2 × 12–15 |

Simple on purpose: 6 movements, every muscle group, technique front and center. Log your weights every session.

---

## Routine 4 — Gym, Intermediate (classic ABC split, 4–6x/week)

**A — Chest, Shoulders, and Triceps:** Barbell Bench Press 4×6–10 · Incline Dumbbell Press 3×8–12 · Machine Chest Fly 3×10–15 · Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press 3×8–12 · Side Lateral Raise 3×12–15 · Rope Triceps Pushdown 3×10–15.

**B — Back, Traps, and Biceps:** Pullups (or Lat Pulldown) 4×6–10 · Bent Over Barbell Row 3×8–12 · Seated Cable Row 3×8–12 · Face Pull 3×12–15 · Barbell Curl 3×8–12 · Cable Rope Hammer Curl 2×10–12.

**C — Full Legs:** Barbell Squat 4×6–10 · Leg Press 3×10–12 · Romanian Deadlift 3×8–12 · Leg Extension 2×12–15 · Seated Leg Curl 2×12–15 · Standing Machine Calf Raise 4×12–20.

## Routine 5 — Fat Loss and Conditioning (circuit, 3–4x/week)

Circuit: run through all 6 exercises back to back, 30–45 s of work and 15–30 s to transition; rest 2 min and repeat for **3 to 4 rounds**.

1. Jump Squats (or light Bodyweight Squats)
2. Pushups (any variation)
3. Mountain Climbers
4. Bent Over Dumbbell Row (light load)
5. Bicycle Crunches (air bike)
6. Jump Rope (or running in place)

Finish with 10–20 min of light steady-state cardio (treadmill, bike, elliptical). Pair it with a moderate calorie deficit — training doesn't make up for diet.

---

## Routine 6 — Basic Strength (barbell, 3x/week, alternating A/B)

**Workout A:** Barbell Squat 5×5 · Barbell Bench Press 5×5 · Bent Over Barbell Row 5×5.
**Workout B:** Barbell Squat 5×5 · Standing Military Press 5×5 · Barbell Deadlift 1×5 (one heavy top set).

Alternate A and B (week 1: A-B-A; week 2: B-A-B). Start with comfortable weights and add 5 lb (2.5 kg) per session as long as you can complete all the reps — when you stall 3 sessions in a row, drop 10% and build back up.

---

## How to Choose

- **Never trained before:** Routine 1 (home) or 3 (gym).
- **Been training 6+ months:** Routine 2 (home) or 4 (gym).
- **Main goal is fat loss:** Routine 5, with daily walks on your off days.
- **Want to get strong on the basics:** Routine 6.
